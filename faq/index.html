Frequently Asked Questions

What is PDR?

PDR stands for Paintless Dent Repair — a specialized, non-invasive method of restoring a vehicle’s body to its original condition without the need for paint, fillers, or panel replacements. Using precision tools, skilled technicians carefully manipulate the metal from behind the panel, massaging the dent out until it seamlessly blends back into its factory shape.

Because PDR preserves your vehicle’s original paint finish, it offers multiple advantages over traditional body shop repairs.

What to Do if You Have Hail Damage?

  1. File a claim with your insurance – Let them know your vehicle was damaged in a hailstorm.
  2. Contact Blueprint Hail Team – Schedule a quick vehicle inspection and drop-off.
  3. We handle the rest – From working with your insurance to completing high-quality Paintless Dent Repairs, we take care of the entire process.

Does Blueprint Hail Team Work with Insurance?

Yes — we work directly with all major insurance companies to make the repair process as smooth as possible for you.

What Is the Turnaround Time?

Hail Damage Repairs

Most hail damage repairs take about 1–2 weeks from start to finish. Timing depends on how quickly your insurance company approves the claim and if any parts need to be ordered.

Smaller Dents

Repairs for smaller dents or door dings are typically completed in 1–3 days once your vehicle is in the shop.

We don’t guarantee an exact completion date up front, but we stay in contact with you throughout the process so you always know the status of your vehicle. Our goal is to return your car in top condition without the stress.

Will My Vehicle Lose Its Value?

When hail damage or dents are left unrepaired, your vehicle’s value can drop significantly — both in resale and trade-in offers.

However, with Paintless Dent Repair (PDR), we restore your vehicle’s panels to their original factory shape without repainting or using fillers. This means:

  • Your factory finish is preserved, which is a key factor in maintaining value.
  • Repairs are virtually undetectable, so buyers and appraisers won’t see evidence of damage.
  • You avoid “accident” or “damage” flags that can show up when panels are replaced or painted.
